Search engine optimization sounds complicated. But for small businesses, 80% of the value comes from getting 15 basic things right.

This checklist covers the highest-impact SEO fixes you can implement today — no technical expertise required.

## On-Page SEO Fixes (Do These First)

![](assets/images/uploads/7.png)

### 1. Write a Title Tag That Includes Your Main Keyword

Every page on your website has a title tag (the text that shows up in Google search results). Make sure it includes what you do and where you do it.

**Bad:** "Home | ABC Company"
**Good:** "Plumbing Services in Charlotte, NC | ABC Plumbing"

### 2. Write a Meta Description That Drives Clicks

The meta description is the 2-line preview under your title in search results. Write it like an ad: what you offer + why choose you + CTA.

**Example:** "Licensed plumbing services in Charlotte. Same-day emergency repairs, upfront pricing, 500+ 5-star reviews. Call for a free estimate."

### 3. Use One H1 Tag Per Page

Your main page heading (H1) should clearly state what the page is about, including your key term. Only use one H1 per page.

### 4. Add Alt Text to Every Image

Google can't "see" images. Alt text describes what's in the image and helps it appear in image search results. Include relevant keywords naturally.

### 5. Make Your Site Mobile-Friendly

Over 60% of Google searches happen on mobile. Test your site at Google's Mobile-Friendly Test tool. Fix any issues it flags.

## Google Business Profile Optimization

### 6. Claim and Verify Your Google Business Profile

If you haven't done this, stop reading and do it now. It's free and it's the single most important thing a local business can do for visibility.

### 7. Fill Out Every Field Completely

Business name, address, phone, website, hours, categories, attributes, services — fill in everything. Profiles with complete information rank higher.

### 8. Add 10+ High-Quality Photos

Businesses with photos receive 42% more requests for directions and 35% more click-throughs to their websites, according to Google.

### 9. Get 10+ Reviews (and Respond to All of Them)

Reviews are the #1 local ranking factor after your Google Business Profile itself. Ask every happy client. Respond to every review — positive and negative.

### 10. Post Weekly Updates to Your Profile

Google Business lets you publish posts (updates, offers, events). Businesses that post weekly rank higher in local search. Set a reminder every Monday.

## Technical SEO Basics

### 11. Check Your Site Speed

Run your URL through Google PageSpeed Insights. Aim for a score above 70 on mobile. Common fixes: compress images, enable browser caching, minimize CSS/JS.

### 12. Install an SSL Certificate (HTTPS)

If your website URL starts with "http://" instead of "https://", Google penalizes you in rankings. Most hosting providers offer free SSL certificates.

### 13. Fix Broken Links

Use a free tool like Broken Link Checker to find and fix any dead links on your site. Broken links hurt both user experience and search rankings.

### 14. Submit Your Sitemap to Google Search Console

Google Search Console is free. It shows you how Google sees your site, what keywords you're ranking for, and any errors. Submit your sitemap so Google can index your pages.

### 15. Ensure Your NAP Is Consistent Everywhere

NAP = Name, Address, Phone number. This must be identical across your website, Google Business Profile, Yelp, Facebook, and every other listing. Inconsistencies tank your local rankings.

## The SEO Priority Matrix

Not all fixes are equal. Here's where to focus first:

**Highest Impact (Do This Week):**

* Claim/optimize Google Business Profile
* Fix title tags and meta descriptions
* Get 5 new reviews

**Medium Impact (Do This Month):**

* Add alt text to all images
* Fix broken links
* Improve site speed

**Ongoing:**

* Post weekly to Google Business
* Publish blog content targeting local keywords
* Build reviews consistently

## The Bottom Line

SEO isn't magic. It's a checklist. Work through these 15 items systematically, and you'll see improvements within 30-60 days.

The businesses that rank on page 1 aren't necessarily better at what they do. They're just better at telling Google what they do.
