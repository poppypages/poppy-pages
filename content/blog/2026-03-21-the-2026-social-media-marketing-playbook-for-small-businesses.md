Social media marketing doesn't have to be overwhelming. The businesses that win aren't spending 4 hours a day on content. They follow a system.

This playbook covers which platforms matter, what to post, and how to turn followers into leads — all tailored for small businesses.

## Choosing the Right Platforms

The biggest mistake small businesses make: trying to be everywhere. Pick 1-2 platforms and go deep.

![](assets/images/uploads/platform-playbook.png)

**LinkedIn:** Best for B2B services, consultants, agencies, and professional services. Where decision-makers spend their time.

**Instagram:** Best for visual businesses — restaurants, retailers, fitness, beauty, real estate. Product showcase platform.

**Facebook:** Best for local businesses targeting 35+ demographics. Facebook Groups and Marketplace are underrated.

**TikTok:** Best for businesses that can create quick, educational videos. Growing fast but requires consistent video production.

**Google Business Profile:** Not technically social media, but the highest-ROI "platform" for any local business. Optimize this before anything else.

### How to Choose

1. Where do your current clients spend time online?
2. What content format are you willing to create consistently?
3. Where are your competitors most active?

Answer those three questions and you'll know where to focus.

## The Content System That Eliminates Guesswork

![]()

### Pick 3-4 Content Pillars

These are the topics you'll always post about. Everything maps to a pillar.

**Example for a marketing agency:**

* Pillar 1: Tactical marketing tips (how-tos)
* Pillar 2: Tools and resource recommendations
* Pillar 3: Frameworks and reusable systems
* Pillar 4: Industry trends and data

### Follow a Weekly Rotation

Assign a content type to each day:

* Monday: Actionable list
* Tuesday: Carousel/infographic
* Wednesday: Industry insight or hot take
* Thursday: Tool recommendation
* Friday: Free resource/lead magnet

### Batch Create Monthly

Sit down once a month for 2 hours:

1. Generate 20 topic ideas using your pillars
2. Write the posts (use AI for drafts, edit with your voice)
3. Create any visual content in Canva
4. Schedule everything using a free tool (Buffer, Later)

## Turning Followers Into Leads: The 3-Step Funnel

Followers are not clients. You need a system to move them from "interested" to "buying."

![](assets/images/uploads/8.15.5-conversion-funnel.png)

### Step 1: Value Content (80% of Posts)

Teach, share frameworks, recommend tools. Build trust and authority. No selling.

### Step 2: Lead Magnets (15% of Posts)

Offer something free and valuable: checklists, templates, guides. CTA: "Comment \[KEYWORD] and I'll DM it to you."

This does two things: identifies interested leads AND generates engagement that extends your reach.

### Step 3: Direct CTA (5% of Posts)

Once you've built trust and identified warm leads through lead magnets, offer a clear next step: "Book a free 15-minute strategy call."

The ratio matters. Sell too often and people unfollow. Never sell and you don't get clients.

## The Engagement Strategy That Outperforms Paid Ads

![](assets/images/uploads/15-minute-daily-social-engagement-sprint.png)

Organic engagement consistently outperforms paid ads for small businesses with limited budgets. Here's the daily routine:

**15 minutes per day:**

* Minutes 1-5: Comment thoughtfully on 5 posts from potential clients
* Minutes 6-10: Respond to all comments on your recent posts
* Minutes 11-15: Send 3 personalized connection requests

This costs $0 and generates more qualified leads than a $500 ad budget.

### Why It Works

Paid ads create awareness. Organic engagement creates relationships. Relationships create clients who refer more clients.

## Measuring What Matters



![](assets/images/uploads/social-media-success-scorecard.png)

Track these monthly:

| Metric               | What It Tells You                  |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Profile views        | Is your content driving curiosity? |
| Post impressions     | Are you reaching more people?      |
| Engagement rate      | Is your content resonating?        |
| DMs received         | Are you generating interest?       |
| Lead magnet comments | Is your audience ready to engage?  |
| Website clicks       | Is social media driving traffic?   |



Ignore: follower count as a standalone metric. 500 engaged followers beat 5,000 passive ones.

## The Bottom Line

Social media marketing for small businesses comes down to three things: pick the right platform, follow a system, and show up consistently.

You don't need a marketing team. You don't need a big budget. You need 30 minutes a day and a plan.
