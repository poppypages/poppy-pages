Social media reach is rented. You don't own your Instagram followers or LinkedIn connections. The platform does.

Email is owned. Your email list is yours. No algorithm decides who sees your message. No platform can take it away.

For small businesses, email marketing delivers $36 for every $1 spent — the highest ROI of any marketing channel.

This guide covers how to start from zero.

## Why Email Still Beats Social Media for Sales

Social media is where people discover you. Email is where they buy from you.

The data is clear:
- Email conversion rates are 3-5x higher than social media
- 72% of consumers prefer email for business communication
- You own the list — no algorithm changes can reduce your reach

Think of social media as the top of the funnel and email as the bottom.

## Building Your First Email List

### Method 1: Lead Magnets
Create a valuable free resource and require an email to access it.

**High-converting lead magnets for small businesses:**
- Checklists (e.g., "25-Point Brand Audit")
- Templates (e.g., "30-Day Content Calendar")
- Guides (e.g., "The SEO Starter Guide")
- Toolkits (e.g., "Free Marketing Toolkit")

### Method 2: Website Opt-In Forms
Place email signup forms in three strategic locations:
1. Homepage hero section (above the fold)
2. Blog post footer (capture readers who found value)
3. Exit-intent popup (catch visitors before they leave)

### Method 3: LinkedIn Lead Magnets
Post about your free resource on LinkedIn. CTA: "Comment 'GUIDE' to get it." Then DM them with a signup link.

This method builds both your LinkedIn engagement AND your email list simultaneously.

## The 3 Emails Every Small Business Should Send

### 1. Welcome Sequence (Automated, 3-5 emails)
Triggered when someone joins your list:
- **Email 1 (Day 0):** Deliver the lead magnet + introduce yourself
- **Email 2 (Day 2):** Share your best piece of content
- **Email 3 (Day 4):** Provide another valuable resource
- **Email 4 (Day 7):** Soft CTA — offer a free consultation

### 2. Weekly Value Email
One email per week with genuinely useful content. Keep it simple:
- One tip or insight
- One link to your latest blog post or resource
- One question to drive replies

### 3. Monthly Offer Email
Once per month, make a direct offer:
- Limited-time promotion
- New service announcement
- Seasonal package

The ratio: 4 value emails for every 1 sales email.

## Writing Emails That Get Opened

**Subject Line Rules:**
- Keep it under 50 characters
- Use numbers when possible ("5 tips" not "some tips")
- Create curiosity ("The one mistake killing your leads")
- Avoid spam triggers (ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, "FREE!!!!")

**Body Copy Rules:**
- Write like you're emailing one person, not a list
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- One CTA per email
- Add a P.S. line (it's the second most-read part of any email)

## The Welcome Sequence Template

Here's a plug-and-play template:

**Email 1: "Here's your [resource]"**
- Deliver what you promised
- Introduce who you are in 2 sentences
- Tell them what to expect from your emails

**Email 2: "The biggest mistake [audience] makes"**
- Share one valuable insight
- Link to a blog post or resource
- Ask a question to encourage replies

**Email 3: "[Resource] you might have missed"**
- Share another free resource or your best content
- Mention how it solves a specific problem

**Email 4: "Quick question"**
- Ask about their biggest challenge
- Offer a free 15-min call
- Keep it short and personal

## The Bottom Line

You don't need a big list to make email marketing work. You need a list of the right people, a consistent sending schedule, and content that makes them glad they subscribed.

Start with one lead magnet, one signup form, and one weekly email. Build from there.
